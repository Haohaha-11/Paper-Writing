# IEEE TMI Notes

Last checked: 2026-07-15

IEEE TMI should be modeled as a first-class journal target, not as a generic IEEE template preset.

## Official Author Surface

The official TMI site links these author resources:

- Instruction to Authors
- FAQ for Authors
- Submission Checklist
- Review Article Guidelines
- ScholarOne manuscript submission
- IEEE manuscript templates through the IEEE template selector

The submission checklist is the strongest source for automated checks in v0.1.

## Checklist Items To Encode

General checks:

- manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere;
- conference extension is disclosed in the cover letter and differences are highlighted;
- co-author emails and affiliations are accurate;
- no prohibited author list issue;
- no reviewer suggestions in cover letter;
- sponsor/financial support acknowledgments, affiliations, and emails appear in the first-page footnote per IEEE templates;
- references use the required author-name format;
- allowed supplement types are graphical abstract, video, media, dataset, code, and published conference paper being extended;
- figures and tables are placed in the main text;
- no author biographies in the manuscript;
- double-column, single-spaced formatting with integrated figures;
- initial PDF is below 40 MB.

Paper-type checks:

- regular/special issue initial submissions: 10 pages, including references;
- challenge paper initial submissions: 14 pages, including references;
- invited review papers require prior proposal/approval;
- revision/resubmission packages need clean copy, response to reviewers, and marked-up copy.

Conference-extension checks:

- cite and describe the prior conference paper;
- explain new and substantial extensions in the cover letter;
- upload the original conference paper as supporting material;
- keep overlap below the stated CrossCheck threshold.

## Writing Skills Needed

TMI-specific writing skills should check more than style:

- clinical or biomedical motivation is explicit and not overstated;
- dataset provenance, modality, cohort, split protocol, and privacy/consent/IRB language are present where applicable;
- validation matches the claim: retrospective, prospective, external validation, cross-site, reader study, challenge benchmark, or ablation-only;
- challenge papers document challenge objective, gold standard, evaluation/testing setup, review process, proceedings link, and participant permissions;
- limitations include deployment, generalization, demographics, scanner/protocol variation, annotation quality, and clinical workflow caveats when relevant.
